What is GCD?

The GCD (Greatest Common Divisor) of two numbers is the largest number that divides both of them without leaving a remainder. For example, the GCD of 12 and 18 is 6.

Example

Consider the numbers 12 and 18:

  • Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
  • Factors of 18: 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18

The common factors are 1, 2, 3, and 6. The greatest of these is 6, so the GCD of 12 and 18 is 6.
