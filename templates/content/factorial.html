What is a Factorial?

A factorial is the product of all positive integers less than or equal to a given number. The factorial of a number 'n' is denoted as 'n!'. For example, the factorial of 5 (5!) is:

5! = 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 120

How to Calculate a Factorial?

To calculate the factorial of a number, you start by multiplying the number by each integer less than it, down to 1. Here's a simple step-by-step example for calculating 4!:

  • 4! = 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 24

For larger numbers, the same principle applies. The factorial of 6 would be calculated as:

  • 6! = 6 × 5 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 720

How Factorial is Used in Code

In programming, calculating the factorial of a number is a common task. Here’s how it works:

1. *Input*: A user provides a number (n).
2. *Factorial Calculation*: The program multiplies all integers from 1 to n together.
3. *Output*: The result is displayed to the user.

Factorial calculations are often used in algorithms related to permutations, combinations, and recursive functions.
