What is a Prime Number?

A prime number is a number greater than 1 that has no divisors other than 1 and itself. For example:

2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17...

How to Determine Prime Numbers?

To determine whether a number is prime, follow these steps:

  • Check if the number is greater than 1.
  • Divide it by numbers from 2 to its square root.
  • If no divisor other than 1 and itself exists, the number is prime.

For example:

  • 7 → Divisors: 1, 7 → Prime
  • 9 → Divisors: 1, 3, 9 → Not Prime

How Prime Numbers are Used in Code

Prime number calculations are commonly implemented in programming. Here’s how:

1. *Input*: A user specifies a number to check or a range to generate prime numbers.
2. *Calculation*: The program checks divisibility to determine primes.
3. *Output*: The result is displayed to the user.

Prime numbers are essential in cryptography, security algorithms, and number theory.
