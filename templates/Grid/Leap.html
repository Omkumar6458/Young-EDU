What is a Leap Year?

A leap year is a year that has 366 days instead of the usual 365 days. This extra day, February 29, is added to the calendar to keep it synchronized with the Earth's orbit around the Sun. For example:

Leap Years: 2020, 2024, 2028...

How to Determine a Leap Year?

To determine if a year is a leap year, follow these rules:

  • The year is evenly divisible by 4.
  • If the year is divisible by 100, it must also be divisible by 400.
  • Otherwise, it is not a leap year.

For example:

  • 2000 → Divisible by 4 and 400 → Leap Year
  • 1900 → Divisible by 4 but not 400 → Not a Leap Year
  • 2024 → Divisible by 4 → Leap Year

How Leap Years are Used in Code

Leap year calculations are often implemented in programming. Here’s how:

1. *Input*: A user enters a year to check.
2. *Calculation*: The program applies the divisibility rules.
3. *Output*: The result indicates whether the year is a leap year.

Leap year logic is used in calendar systems and date validations.
